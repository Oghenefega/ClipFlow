Thumbnail treatments

Your recordings are 2560×2880, taller than wide. Containers below are drawn to scale. Move your mouse across the two scrub tiles above (any row thumbnail) to feel the hover-scrub. Thumb width comes from each file's real aspect ratio, so a 16:9 user gets a wide thumb and you get a narrow one; nothing is hardcoded.

Today: 16:9 box, cover-cropCURRENT
The 8:9 frame is scaled to fill a 16:9 box, so half of every frame is cropped away. This is the squish you're seeing.
Native aspect, fixed heightVARIANT A + B
Whole frame visible, tiny footprint. Height is fixed per row; width follows the file's real aspect from the probe data.
Filmstrip
Shows the whole video at a glance with no hover needed, but eats ~150px of row width. Better fit for a detail or peek view than the list.
Hover-scrubRECOMMENDED
Mouse position picks the frame, like YouTube previews. Replaces the timed crossfade: you control where you look. Uses the same frames we already extract.
Selection is live in this mock: click checkboxes (rows or a session header) and the bottom bar switches from "Rename All" to selected-count mode. Shift-click range select and Ctrl+A would come along in the real build. The History and Manage sub-tabs are untouched by this redesign; the slim header strip replaces the four stat cards and the watching banner. Rename All ignores hidden files exactly as today.
